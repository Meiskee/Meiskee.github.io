Acts Of Service

"Act of service" is one of the five love languages introduced by Gary Chapman. This concept refers to the way a person shows love and care through tangible actions to help others. These actions can be small gestures, such as helping with household chores, providing support when someone is in need, preparing meals, or taking time to listen and accompany others. For those who feel loved through "acts of service," these actions are very meaningful and demonstrate care and commitment.

Words Of affirmation

"Words of affirmation" is one of the five love languages introduced by Gary Chapman. This concept refers to the way a person expresses love and care through positive words, praise, or verbal support. For those who have this love language, expressions of affection, recognition, and encouragement from loved ones are very meaningful. Examples include saying "I'm proud of you," giving compliments for achievements, or simply expressing feelings of love directly. Sincere words can strengthen relationships and make someone feel valued and loved.

Quality Time

"Quality time" is one of the five love languages introduced by Gary Chapman. This concept refers to the way a person shows love and care through meaningful presence and interaction. For those who have this love language, spending undistracted time together is very important. This can involve talking, engaging in activities together, or simply enjoying each other’s company. The focus and full attention during these moments make them feel valued and loved.
